How Social Spark Studio Works

A seven-phase pipeline that turns one YouTube video into a full, voice-aligned social media campaign — end to end, without copy-paste or prompt writing.

Phase 1 — MessageDNA

Run once per founder. Saved forever.

MessageDNA is the foundation of every campaign. You answer four blocks of interview questions — audience, offer, voice, and positioning — spoken aloud or typed. A Google Gemini agent listens, reflects back what it heard, and asks you to confirm each block before moving on.

The output is a structured JSON file (message_dna_output.json) containing your voice words, forbidden phrases, content pillars, visual metaphors, and contrarian beliefs. Every downstream agent reads this file to stay in your voice — not a generic AI voice.

Input: Voice interview or text answers

Output: data/message_dna_output.json

Tech: Google ADK · Gemini 2.5 Flash · Chirp 3 HD TTS · Speech-to-Text v2

Phase 2 — Campaign Brief

One brief per campaign.

The Campaign Brief scopes a single campaign: what you're promoting, who it's for, what the CTA is, and which YouTube video to draw content from. A Gemini-powered brief agent interviews you through five structured questions and writes the brief for you.

Input: Campaign goal, offer, audience, CTA, YouTube URL

Output: data/campaign_brief.json

Tech: Google ADK · Gemini 2.5 Flash

Phase 3 — YouTube Transcript

Any public YouTube video, any length.

Paste a YouTube URL and the transcript runner fetches the full caption track, segments it into timestamped chunks, and saves a structured JSON file. Each segment records the text, start time, and word count so downstream agents can generate precise timestamp URLs back to the source video.

Input: YouTube URL (from Campaign Brief)

Output: data/transcript_output.json

Tech: youtube-transcript-api · FastAPI background task

Phase 4 — Moment Selection

AI-scored, founder-voice-filtered.

A Gemini agent reads the full transcript against your MessageDNA and Campaign Brief, then scores every segment for social worthiness: Is it quotable? Does it match a content pillar? Is there a strong hook? It selects up to 9 high-signal moments and explains why each one works.

Each moment includes a verbatim quote, a YouTube timestamp URL, a platform recommendation, and a note on why it resonates — the raw material the caption agent works from in Phase 5.

Input: transcript + MessageDNA + Campaign Brief

Output: data/moments_output.json

Tech: Google ADK · Gemini 2.5 Flash · ~30–90 s

Phase 5 — Post Drafts

Two agents. Up to 15 posts. One pass.

Two agents run back-to-back. The Caption Agent drafts up to 15 posts across three content types: video clips (built from transcript moments), image posts (built from MessageDNA content pillars), and text quotes (pulled from strong transcript language). Every caption is written in your confirmed voice using your phrases-to-use list and avoiding your phrases-to-avoid list.

The Hashtag Agent then runs a second pass and assigns three tiers of hashtags to every post: broad reach, niche audience, and brand-specific — all sourced from your MessageDNA content pillars and audience profile.

Input: moments + MessageDNA + Campaign Brief

Output: data/posts_output.json

Tech: Google ADK · Gemini 2.5 Flash · 2 agents · ~60 s

Phase 6 — Image Generation

~20 seconds per image.

For every image_post in your campaign, the pipeline sends a visual prompt — built from your MessageDNA visual metaphors and the post's content pillar — to Google Imagen. The generated images are saved locally and served directly in the UI via /api/data/images/.

If Imagen returns a safety block on any image, the runner automatically falls back to imagen-4.0-fast-generate-001 before marking the post as failed. You can retry individual failed posts from the Posts page.

Input: posts_output.json (image_post entries only)

Output: data/generated_images/*.png

Tech: Google Imagen 4.0 · Vertex AI · ~20 s/image

Phase 7 — CSV Export

Scheduler-ready in one click.

The export runner writes every post to a single CSV file with columns matching the import format for Buffer, Hypefury, and SparkCircle. Video clip posts include a YouTube timestamp URL as the media asset. Image posts reference the generated image path. Text quote posts export clean.

Input: posts_output.json

Output: data/social-spark-<campaign>-<date>.csv

Columns: post_number, platform, content_type, caption, hashtags, image_url, video_url, CTA

Powered by a multi-agent ADK workflow

Social Spark Studio is built on the Google Agent Development Kit (ADK) — a framework for composing specialised AI agents into coordinated pipelines. Rather than sending one large prompt to a single model, each phase is handled by a dedicated agent with a specific role, instruction set, and output schema.
